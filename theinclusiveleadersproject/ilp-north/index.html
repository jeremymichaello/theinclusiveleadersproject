North Quadrant – Sensing

Theme: Identity, Story, Context, Purpose

Module 1.1 – Foundational Identity: Discover Your Team DNA

Explore the heartbeat of your team by surfacing the personal and collective values that define who you are. This module centers on the internal architecture of identity.

Acquired Skills:
  • Core values articulation: Naming and expressing personal and shared beliefs.
  • Ethical self-assessment: Reflecting on how values inform decisions.
  • Personal–team identity mapping: Connecting individual identities to team culture.

Takeaway: Facilitates a group dialogue and visual chart of team values and their impact.

Module 1.2 – Narrative Memory: Craft Your Collective Story

Trace your team’s journey through personal and collective experiences. This module explores the emotional roots and meaning in shared history.

Acquired Skills:
  • Narrative construction: Structuring a coherent team story.
  • Collective memory weaving: Incorporating diverse perspectives.
  • Emotional meaning-making: Recognizing and communicating story significance.

Takeaway: Leads the team through timeline creation of key milestones and stories.

Module 1.3 – Contextual Embedding: Navigate Your Environment

View the systems that surround your work and how they shape your experience.

Acquired Skills:
  • Systems awareness: Identifying external conditions and variables.
  • External analysis: Evaluating socio-political and institutional contexts.
  • Strategic positioning: Adjusting team identity to fit and resist external forces.

Takeaway: Creates an external forces map highlighting barriers and supports.

Module 1.4 – Purpose Activation: Ignite Your Mission

Turn shared meaning into forward motion by crafting a team mission and sustaining it through ritual.

Acquired Skills:
  • Mission drafting: Writing resonant team purpose statements.
  • Purpose alignment: Ensuring mission matches lived team experience.
  • Ritual facilitation: Designing symbolic actions to reinforce purpose.

Takeaway: Facilitates a collaborative session that results in a co-authored mission statement and launch ritual.

Module 1.5 – Value Networks: Master Your Connections

Shift from internal clarity to external relationships. Map key connections and flows of value.

Acquired Skills:
  • Stakeholder analysis: Identifying key partners and roles.
  • Value chain mapping: Tracing where and how energy, trust, and resources flow.
  • Relational system thinking: Understanding networks as dynamic ecosystems.

Takeaway: Constructs a stakeholder influence and value map with team input.

Module 1.6 – Relational Dynamics: Strengthen Your Bonds

Examine the interpersonal architecture of the team. Strengthen connection, trust, and mutual understanding.

Acquired Skills:
  • Relational assessment: Diagnosing the current health of key interactions.
  • Communication style fluency: Navigating different ways of expressing and receiving.
  • Trust-building strategies: Applying tools to increase psychological safety.

Takeaway: Co-facilitates a relational mapping exercise with reflection and strategy alignment.

Module 1.7 – Cognitive Resonance: Align Your Mindset

Surface the internal beliefs and thought patterns shaping the team. Align mindset with purpose.

Acquired Skills:
  • Bias identification: Recognizing shared cognitive tendencies.
  • Mindset mapping: Visualizing how beliefs influence behavior.
  • Collective cognitive realignment: Shifting shared assumptions for growth.

Takeaway: Leads a journaling and team discussion exercise on cognitive patterns and alternatives.

Module 1.8 – Vision Realization: Achieve Your Dreams

Translate everything you’ve built into a compelling, shared future vision.

Acquired Skills:
  • Vision-setting: Imagining long-term aspirations together.
  • Strategic foresight: Anticipating challenges and designing toward opportunity.
  • Long-term ritual planning: Creating rhythm and commitment to sustain momentum.

Takeaway: Drafts a vision statement and leads a quarterly vision review ritual planning session.
