The Empathy Bridge

Empathy is not about feeling sorry for someone; it's about striving to understand their perspective. For leaders, it's a critical skill for building trust, fostering collaboration, and navigating conflict. It requires active listening and a genuine curiosity about others' experiences.

Goleman's Levels of Empathy

Cognitive Empathy

"I understand your perspective."

The ability to understand another person's point of view on an intellectual level. You can see their mental models and how they see the world.

Emotional Empathy

"I feel with you."

The ability to feel what another person feels. This creates a sense of rapport and deep connection through shared emotions.

Compassionate Empathy

"I am moved to help you."

Understanding and feeling someone's situation, and then being spontaneously moved to act and help them if needed.

The Ripple Effect

Our individual actions as leaders create ripples that can either reinforce or challenge existing systems. Championing inclusion means recognizing our power to influence norms and policies. It's about moving from being a passive bystander to an active ally for equity.
